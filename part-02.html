Architecture Brief

From Knowledge
to Memory

Every enterprise data strategy of the last decade was built on a single premise: better data produces better decisions. That premise is incomplete.

Better data produces better inputs. What happens to those inputs — how they are interpreted, by whom, under what definitions, in what context, against what prior judgment — is where intelligence actually lives. And almost no enterprise has a systematic way to preserve it.

This is the argument for corporate memory: not a feature of the data stack, but a different class of architecture entirely.
