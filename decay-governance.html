Decay is not deletion. It is lifecycle management. Context that was once essential to a decision should never disappear, but it should not continue driving live reasoning after the conditions it described have resolved.

Decay Governance

Context Relevance Across Time

Manages the lifecycle of contextual facts. The problem is vertical, context that was true becoming less relevant as the world it described changes.

ProblemRelevance degradation over time
DirectionVertical, across time
UrgencyManaged, predictable and gradual

Without itStale context contaminates live reasoning

Drift and decay share the same failure signature: nothing breaks visibly. The definitions quietly diverge. The context quietly expires. The agent quietly reasons on both.

Context Durability

Not all context expires at the same rate. The governance cadence for each record should be calibrated to its natural durability — a competitor incentive campaign and an IC investment thesis require fundamentally different review windows.

Competitor incentive campaign in submarket 2–4 months
Property management transition context 6–12 months
Submarket supply wave (construction pipeline) 18–30 months
Interest rate environment and financing conditions 24–48 months
JV partnership structure and governance terms Fund life
Asset classification rationale (core / value-add) Hold period +
IC approval rationale and investment thesis Permanent

The Three Decay States

State 01, Active

Currently Relevant

Influences live reasoning. Visible to agents and analysts when synthesizing recommendations or evaluating performance. Context remains Active until an explicit trigger moves it, a resolution event, a time threshold, or a conditions-changed flag.

Duration: weeks → years depending on context type
State 02, Archived

No Longer Current

Excluded from live reasoning. Fully preserved for lineage reconstruction and historical analysis. Carries its full provenance: what it was, when it was active, what triggered the transition, and what replaced it. Queryable, just not present in current-state views.

Retention: indefinite, for decision lineage integrity
State 03, Deprecated

Superseded

Replaced by new context. The transition point is recorded. The deprecated record points forward to what replaced it; the replacement points back. This is a labeled handoff, not erasure. The bidirectional pointer preserves the narrative of how understanding evolved.

Pointer: bidirectional, timestamped, preserved
Why Deletion Is the Wrong Answer Stale context was the episodic record at the moment of a past decision. Deleting it makes that decision permanently uninterpretable. The recommendation made in August 2024 cannot be reconstructed without the competitive context that was active, and then resolved, in that period. Deletion destroys lineage. Archival preserves it.
Real Estate Application
RE Application

When Drift and Decay Interact

The most architecturally complex scenario is when both problems occur simultaneously — a definition has drifted and the context it was applied to is no longer current. This is not uncommon in long-hold real estate portfolios where operating models have changed, counterparties have turned over, and market conditions have shifted through a full cycle.

The design principle: drift resolution and decay archival must be coordinated, not independent. When a drift event is resolved with a formal version boundary, that boundary should trigger a decay state review of any context tagged with the prior definition version. The version boundary marks the point where semantic meaning changed — the decay review determines which contextual facts were rendered obsolete by that same shift.

Scenario The definition of "effective rent" in a JV portfolio was updated in Q2 2023 to include tenant improvement amortization (previously excluded). This is a drift resolution — version boundary recorded, both parties aligned. But it also means that every contextual fact tagged under the prior effective rent definition — submarket rent benchmarks, comparable lease analyses, renewal rate assessments — may now be reading a different concept than the one they were originally calibrated against. The decay review asks: do those contextual facts need to be archived and re-evaluated under the new definition, or are they still valid as-is? Without this coordinated review, the context graph carries mixed provenance — some facts computed under v1, some under v2, with no flag distinguishing them.
